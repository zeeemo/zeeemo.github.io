Intro

I’m a Data Scientist - but not by birth; I fell into data by accident. I was at a crossroads in my career and discovered a budding interest at the intersection of business and data. While in class one evening, I was tasked with making a decision in a case study about two racecar business owners. They were struggling to make a decision about whether to race in an upcoming event. If they won, they would win sponsorship money whereas losing meant protecting their brand but losing money and sponsorships.

The decision seemed clear based on the data; the risk of reward was greater than the risk of not trying at all. I decided to race.

But then, plot twist.

My class discovered the dataset we worked with was actually the same dataset NASA scientists used in the Challenger explosion in 1986.

I walked away having experienced this ‘aha’ moment and was amazed how data had been presented and a decision made that ultimately cost seven people their lives. Someone made the conscious decision that lives weren’t worth what it would cost to delay the launch.

As time marched on, I kept returning to this case study and realized my interest in data was no longer just an interest. I began teaching myself Python and Tableau so I could ask more intelligent questions and really understand data. I spent my lunch breaks learning and soon realized I would rather spend more time learning than doing my job. That’s when I decided it was time to explore my options.

Without the formal education, I knew it would be tough to pivot. My background is mostly client-facing/customer service experience. I was working as a technical recruiter at the time and after talking to a few candidates who had completed a coding bootcamp, decided this was the best route for me.

I began at General Assembly in February, 2019 in their Data Science Immservise. During the course, I learned a ton of Python, modeling techniques, and how to apply data science to real world problems. I completed 20+ labs, 6 projects, and spent over 500 hours in this course.

Three months later, I emerged with the skillset of a Data Scientist and I couldn’t be more ready to tackle the world of data. Thanks for joining me.

/ zmo

Portfolio

Please see my GitHub for more information on my projects and older work

SAT Participation:
Using different methods of exploratory data analysis, compared participation rates among the ACT and SAT over two years in order to suggest where College Board should focus their efforts in order to grow participation rates among students takig the SAT.

Predicting Housing Prices in Ames, Iowa:
Utilized multiple regression in order to predict housing prices. I completed several differnt models in order to learn about feature engineering and machine learning.

Reddit Classification using Natural Language Processing(NLP):
Using Reddit's API, pulled text data from two different subreddits and created an NLP model to correctly predict from which subreddit the text came. This project was aimed at gaining more experience with NLP and the process of working with text data like tokenizing, lemmatizing, etc.

Predicting Disaster Estimates:
Created a model that could accurately predict natural disaster estimates for damage to property when provided zip codes. Also created a GUI and a radial map tool in order to quickly and visually see impact.

Predicting Hospital Readmission with Diabetes:
Using classification and Logistic Regression, created several models to predict the likelihood of patients with Diabetes to be readmitted to hospitals around the US.
